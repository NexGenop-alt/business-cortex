# Business Cortex Client Onboarding Questionnaire

This questionnaire is used to configure Business Cortex for a new client.

The goal is not only to learn what the company does. The goal is to capture how
the company thinks, decides, prioritizes, communicates, sells, serves customers,
handles risk, and defines success so the agent system can operate from day one
with the right context and guardrails.

Use this as a guided interview. The client does not need perfect answers. Raw,
messy answers are acceptable. Business Cortex can convert them into operating
rules, workflows, agent instructions, memory seeds, and approval policies.

# Required Output After Questionnaire Is Completed

After the client completes this questionnaire, convert the answers into:

1. `client_profile.json` — company identity, industry, goals, values, users.
2. `agent_operating_rules.md` — tone, decision style, escalation rules, guardrails.
3. `workflow_map.md` — prioritized workflows and automation candidates.
4. `approval_policy.json` — what can be drafted, recommended, approved, or automated.
5. `integration_plan.md` — Google/Microsoft/CRM/calendar/email/storage connections.
6. `memory_seed.md` — durable company knowledge to load into memory/search.
7. `role_agents.yaml` — sales, assistant, support, operations, back-office role definitions.
8. `first_30_days_plan.md` — implementation sequence and success metrics.

# Interviewer Notes

The most important answers are not always the neat ones. Pay close attention to:

- repeated frustrations,
- hidden workarounds,
- undocumented processes,
- phrases like “we always have to…” or “the problem is…”,
- places where employees rely on one person’s memory,
- decisions that require judgment,
- things the client says are impossible but may only be tool or process limitations.

Business Cortex should not pretend to “think like the owner” automatically.
Instead, it should capture enough operating rules, examples, exceptions,
workflows, and approval boundaries that its behavior becomes increasingly close
to how the company wants decisions made.
